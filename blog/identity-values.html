  • Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.
  • Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

Answers


  • I encountered a situation a while ago when one of my co-worker at the Cafe I used to manage would just serve free coffee to his friends. I understand that this person comes from a place that might think it is not a big issue to be serving coffee that has a minimum cost. In addition, several times happens that a specific coffee was wrongly made as specified or because this was such a busy place that the same coffee will be made twice for the same order, and then will be poured down the drain. Although, I come from a place that believes that every single one of us has a job, therefore a paycheck and we know our limitations. When you go into a Cafe I would think that you intended to go with the purpose of paying for the consumption. Otherwise, you will refrain from attending places that are not on your budget. As a manager, I felt that it was my obligation to make my co-worker aware that there is a cost for making a coffee and that there is also wages to be paid to the Barista, the waiter that will deliver or clear that coffee cup, and the person that will then wash the cup afterwards. Coffees were personally made for their friends, they were not mistaken ones. This all adds on to the fact that I am responsible to create the right and fair environment for everyone. My action was to warn him that he will need to pay for their friends' coffees if he continues to keep giving away coffees. The result was that he stopped doing so since he did not want to pay for this.

  • My culture has influenced the way I relate with people, the kind of language I use and how I behave. Where I come from,South America, we have a very assertive and direct way of expressing ourselves. It might seem a bit confrontational at times to people that come from a different culture but I've been working on finding a middle ground. I truly believe that culture is the society identity and it is what either unites the community or it separates it. Feeling part of a community is to me a very important value, therefore I've been working on my values and beliefs to adjust to the new society I live in.

  3. There was a time, when I was a Manager at the Cafe mentioned earlier, and I started noticing things that could be improved. Such things were for example, rearranging the drinks fridge in a way that every drink will be displayed at the same time that it was easily reachable. Another example was stacking old orders of that same day, in a place that we could refer to if any issues arose during this day. Probably I wouldn’t have taken a different approach now. I feel that communicating to whoever is the boss should be the very first thing.
