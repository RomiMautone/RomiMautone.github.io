What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

This is the article that is going to answer all of your questions about this topic.

First of all, let’s have a brief introduction into each concept.

  • Padding - as defined by W3School, it is the space between the element border and the element component (think of element as a text, image) It is basically the space around your element.

  • Border - it is exactly what you just might think. The element’s box that separates the element’s content from the outer space.

  • Margin - is the space around elements, outside of any defined borders.

Here is a visual image for you:

This is an analogy to help you out:

Let’s say that you have a framed picture hanging at your lounge.
The picture itself will be your element’s content, and the frame which contains it will be its border.
Then the margin will be in this case all of the remaining space around that wall.

Please note: all three properties can be used at the same time for a single element, since they all represent different attributes of the element.

How to specify a value?

  • Border, Margin and Padding can have a single value that applies to all four sides.

  • It can also have different values for each side of the box. These values are indicated from the top side in a clockwise way.
    TOP, RIGHT, BOTTOM, LEFT.

  • It can even have two values that apply the same distance for top and bottom, and same distance for right and left.

  • Or it can have three values, for top, right and left, and bottom

Conclusion:

Padding is the distance between the element’s content and the border.
Border is the frame that contains the element’s content.
And margin is the space outside the element’s border.
