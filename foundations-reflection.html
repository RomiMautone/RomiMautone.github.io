Foundations reflection - Tuesday 27th July 2021

Foundations Reflection

This is Sprint 5 reflections about my 5 weeks journey into Foundations.
Here are some questions I had to reflect upon:


What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?


  • I have learnt about negative self-talk and how we can defeat it.
  • I learnt that encountering challenges in a positive way is crucial in order to defeat them.
  • I learnt about growth mindset vs fixed mindset and how I can modify my neural paths to create a better habit.

What are the roles of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?


Everything. I love the idea of complementing our logical brain using our creative/intuitive side as well.
This core content should be taught from an early age and in all sorts of studying environments.
Struggles are part of our daily life and the information and tools presented here are highly beneficial for any individual.


What has surprised you the most about core learning?


Learning about neuroplasticity and the research made by Carol Dweck. It was quite illuminating and empowering.
Having to learn further about the subject really encouraged me to engage with this growth mindset.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?


There were a few questions, I believe that was during End-of-sprint 1 reflections, that the retrospective was very intense and I felt clueless of how to approach my reflections.
At times it is difficult to find the answers within ourselves.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?


I feel that you are preparing our soft-skills for the real world. I think that being a programmer means being motivated, with a positive and growth mindset.
It also means being creative and able to work in a team and under different circumstances.
This core program makes us understand the need for adaptation.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.


I do not feel that it was a waste of time at all. I am very happy with all of the curriculum in its tech and core subjects.


Thanks for reading,

Romina
